Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of theoretical computer science, high-dimensional statistics, and machine learning. I'm passionate about using mathematical tools to analyze and prove results on machine learning algorithms and models. I particularly enjoy working on problems in optimal transport theory!

I am currently working on two projects. The first project uses optimal transport, stochastic calculus, and calculus of variations for latent trajectory inference of probability distributions. The second project is an independent exploratory project that uses probability theory and mathematical analysis to study Fourier analysis of Boolean functions.

These days, I'm interested in the theory of PDEs and how they connect to other areas of mathematics. In particular, I find it beautiful that the solution to certain PDEs can be formulated as an SDE via the Feynman-Kac formula and that the solution to other PDEs can be formulated as a gradient flow in Wasserstein space.

k-Mixup Regularization for Deep Learning via Optimal Transport
Kristjan Greenewald, Anming Gu, Mikhail Yurochkin, Justin Solomon, Edward Chien
Transactions on Machine Learning Research, 2023.
arXiv / code

Mixup is a regularization technique for training neural networks that perturbs input training data in the direction of other randomly chosen training data. We propose a new variant of mixup that uses optimal transport to perturb training data in the direction of other training data that are more similar to the input data. We show theoretically and experimentally that our method is more effective than mixup at improving generalization performance.
