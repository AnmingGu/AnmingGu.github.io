Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of theoretical computer science, statistics, and machine learning, with a particular emphasis on applying mathematical tools to analyze and prove results on machine learning algorithms.

I am currently working two projects. The first project uses optimal transport and mean-field Langevin dynamics to track and predict the evolution of probability distributions. The second project uses Bernstein polynomials, analysis, and topology to study Boolean functions.
